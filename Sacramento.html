The Uneven Toll of Evictions

The rate and volume of evictions in Sacramento County fell to a low point in 2016, according to data released by Princeton University’s Eviction Lab.

Not since the early 2000s have there been less eviction cases filed and evictions judgements rendered in the county. The data released in April appears to be one of the first attempts to calculate the scale of evictions nationwide.

Although the eviction rate is down in the county overall and the city of Sacramento, it’s a different story when you zoom in to look at some individual places. In some cases the more impoverished a place is the higher the rate of eviction.

The data shows that eviction is more common in some places than others. The community of North Highlands, for example, had about 4 percent of the county’s renter-occupied households but experienced 8.5 percent of the evictions in 2016. And North Highlands only takes in about 3 percent of the county population.

Smaller places like Lemon Hill also experience a disproportionate share of eviction.
